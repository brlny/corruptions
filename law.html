Laws and Agencies Fighting Corruption

The Philippines has several laws that protect public funds and ensure accountability. One of the most important is the Anti-Graft and Corrupt Practices Act, which outlines offenses related to bribery, misuse of money, and abuse of power.

Agencies such as the Commission on Audit (COA) review government spending, while the Office of the Ombudsman investigates public officials accused of wrongdoing. These systems exist to uphold integrity and safeguard taxpayer money.
